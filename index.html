What moved this week

This week's theme was access, not new capability. Anthropic's Fable 5 came back online July 1 after the U.S. lifted its export-control order; Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro is now cleared for a July launch (after slipping from June); and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 stayed limited to vetted organizations. Because nearly every AI authoring, roleplay and video tool runs on one of these models, which models are available shifts week to week — worth watching for anyone building on them. Underneath that, AI roleplay for reps kept consolidating toward pharma-specific, compliance-aware simulation.

AI Watch — Claude, Gemini, OpenAI, Copilot

the big models & the rules

A weekly read on the major AI players — because they're the engines under nearly every tool here.

Anthropic · Claude

Claude Sonnet 5 shipped June 30 — near-top-tier at low cost and now the default on free/pro plans. And Fable 5 came back online July 1 after the U.S. lifted its June export-control order.

Why it matters: The models under many training tools are getting cheaper and, for now, more available.

Google · Gemini

Gemini 3.5 Pro slipped from June to a July launch (still in limited enterprise preview). Google also shipped new image-generation models (Gemini 3 Pro Image / 3.1 Flash Image).

Why it matters: A fresh frontier option is close, and cheaper image generation feeds course visuals.

OpenAI · GPT

GPT-5.6 — a Sol / Terra / Luna family — was previewed to government-vetted organizations ahead of a broader July release. A cheaper "Terra" tier is expected.

Why it matters: A lower-cost tier would ease the price of the AI features inside authoring tools.

Microsoft · Copilot

Microsoft 365 Copilot now lets you pick Claude (Opus 4.8) as a model, added computer-using agents in Copilot Studio, and began watermarking AI-generated audio/video.

Why it matters: The everyday productivity assistant is turning agentic — directly relevant to how our teams work day to day.

Policy · Governance

EU AI Act high-risk provisions begin applying August 2, 2026 (CE-marked medical devices/IVDs get an extension to August 2027). FDA and EMA "good AI practice" guidance is pushing pharma to train staff org-wide on AI quality.

Why it matters: "Train the whole org on AI quality" is becoming a defined, fundable need — and a compliance clock.

AI Roleplay & HCP Simulation

the most active area right now

Practice partners and scenario sims that let reps rehearse the doctor (HCP) conversation before the field.

Full platform

Quantified — AI Sales Coaching Platform

Expanded its AI roleplay into a full platform with six AI agents across the rep lifecycle; users self-author compliant simulations without a production cycle. Reports 6× practice frequency and 42% faster ramp.

Why it matters: Roleplay is graduating to an end-to-end platform, and self-authoring compresses the content-build step.

$8M raise

Avarra

Raised $8M for 3D-avatar simulation using a Socratic/reflective model — the rep articulates their own reasoning rather than just receiving a score.

Why it matters: Fresh funding plus a methodology split (coaching vs scoring) signals the category is maturing.

Veeva-integrated

ACTO CxZone

Pharma-specific roleplay that automatically pulls current, approved Veeva materials into scenarios, so reps always practice against compliant content.

Why it matters: Closes the loop between approved content and field practice — key for Veeva shops.

Launch readiness

Awarathon (Trinity Life Sciences)

Two-way physician-persona simulation built for launch readiness, with personas calibrated to specialty and therapeutic area.

Why it matters: A specialist tool for the high-stakes pre-launch certification sprint.

AI Video, Avatars & Multilingual

presenter-led, in any language

Presenter-led and multilingual video generation — "we need this training in 12 languages with a presenter."

Avatars

HeyGen Avatar V

Studio-quality avatar video from a 15-second recording, with Sora, Veo and Kling in one hub; 175+ languages.

Why it matters: Presenter-led multilingual training becomes cheap to produce and refresh.

L&D-native

Synthesia — Expressive Avatars 3.0

More natural avatars plus an in-platform "AI Playground" and interactive courses; 160+ languages. Caveat: still no published HIPAA documentation.

Why it matters: The dominant L&D avatar platform — but check the HIPAA gap before pharma use.

Branching

Colossyan

The one avatar platform with native scenario branching and embedded assessments that report completion to an LMS.

Why it matters: Bridges avatar video and trackable, training-shaped content.

AI Voice & Narration

accuracy & compliance now win

Narration without a studio session is increasingly commoditized, so the differentiators are accuracy, compliance and data residency.

HIPAA · Articulate-native

Murf Gen 2

Claims 99.38% pronunciation accuracy, embeds directly into Articulate 360, and is SOC 2 + HIPAA certified with regional data residency.

Why it matters: Drug-name accuracy plus a compliance posture is exactly what pharma narration needs.

#1 Speech Arena

Inworld Realtime TTS-2

Tops the realtime TTS leaderboard, takes plain-English voice direction, and switches languages mid-stream while keeping the same speaker's voice.

Why it matters: Single-voice consistency across languages is the multilingual unlock.

Open-weight

Voxtral & Qwen3-TTS

Apache-2.0 licensed, self-hostable (Qwen3 runs on 4GB VRAM), roughly half the per-character cost of closed APIs.

Why it matters: A data-residency and cost play where sending audio to a closed cloud is off the table.

Learning Platforms (LMS / LXP)

the AI-agent race

The system layer — where consolidation and AI-agent features are moving fastest.

Enterprise OS

Sana → Workday

Now inside Workday (~$1.1B acquisition), Sana is an AI-native platform whose agent can access Workday data directly.

Why it matters: For Workday shops, learning and enterprise data converge into one agentic layer.

Skills intelligence

Docebo

Acquired 365 Talents for skills intelligence, has AI Agents shipping this fall, and offers a connector that plugs AI assistants into learning data.

Why it matters: An agentic LXP plus skills data now, and faster to deploy than Workday/Sana.

AI overlay

Cornerstone Galaxy

Launched a full AI reinvention (~$1B revenue): automatic development planning, recommendations and content discovery on a mature architecture.

Why it matters: The fit for established-LMS orgs wanting an AI overlay, not a replacement.

Sales Enablement & Commercial Content

compliance automation + field AI

Where reps find and use the right asset — and where compliance automation and field-facing AI are reshaping what "training" covers.

Compliance · Live Jun 23

Veeva Falcon MLR

Automated review of promotional/medical materials against approved labels inside PromoMats; claims a 70%+ reduction in manual review labor within five years.

Why it matters: It reviews finished materials — it shifts where human effort sits rather than removing it.

Merger completed

Seismic (+ Highspot) · Allego

The Highspot–Seismic merger completed, raising a migration question for Highspot customers; Allego (a Gartner leader with FDA 21 CFR Part 11 support) is the stable alternative.

Why it matters: In life sciences, the compliance credentials (21 CFR Part 11) are the deciding factor.

Live field AI

Sanofi "Concierge for Field"

Sanofi deployed a Snowflake AI agent that builds a real-time pre-call plan for each rep — priority physician, engagement summary, prescribing history — before every visit.

Why it matters: Field AI blurs "training" and "in-the-flow support" — product knowledge plus AI fluency is the combined ask.

What We're Doing

the landscape, put to work
Voice · adopting

Moving our narration to ElevenLabs

Standardizing our AI voiceover on ElevenLabs' voices across course production, and retiring our older setup.

Why it matters: Studio-quality, multilingual narration without a studio session — faster course turnaround.

Authoring · new tool

Building with Mighty Studio

Adopting Maestro's newly launched Mighty Studio, which extends what we can design directly inside the courses we build.

Why it matters: More polish and interactivity without leaving our core authoring tool.

Team-wide

Expanding Claude across the team

Rolling Claude out beyond early adopters — into everyday documents, decks, research and production work.

Why it matters: Time savings across the team, plus hands-on AI know-how we bring to client conversations.

Accessibility

Auto-captioning with Storyline AI

Using Storyline's built-in AI assistant to generate and sync video captions in one click, then fine-tune before publishing.

Why it matters: Faster accessibility on video-heavy courses, without the manual captioning grind.

Rethinking delivery

Reevaluating our deliverables

Some deliverables no longer start in Storyline: our API and Market Landscape Grid are now live, hosted web resources. We built dedicated editors for them, so they can be updated quickly — with those editors, or AI coding assistants like Claude Code — instead of rebuilt in Storyline each time.

Why it matters: Faster to update, always current, and shareable by a single link — a new delivery format (this page is one).
